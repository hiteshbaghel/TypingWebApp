Comprehensive   Software   Requirements   Specification   for   a   Government   and   Banking   Examination   Typing   Performance   Platform    The   architecture   of   computerized   recruitment   in   the   Indian   public   sector   has   evolved   into   a   highly   specialized   ecosystem   where   clerical   and   administrative   speed-typing   proficiency   is   no   longer   a   secondary   asset   but   a   primary,   mandatory   qualifying   criterion.   As   administrative   departments   under   the   Staff   Selection   Commission   (SSC),   the   Railway   Recruitment   Board   (RRB),   and   various   public   sector   banking   institutions   move   toward   unified   digital   assessments,   the   demand   for   a   precision-engineered   training   platform   has   intensified.   The   proposed   web-based   software,   hosted   at   webapp.hiteshbaghel.in,   is   designed   to   function   as   an   exhaustive   frontend-only   performance   tool   that   bridges   the   gap   between   casual   typing   practice   and   the   rigorous,   penalty-heavy   environments   of   governmental   skill   tests.   This   report   details   the   functional,   technical,   and   pedagogical   requirements   for   such   a   platform,   emphasizing   the   replication   of   official   marking   systems,   the   integration   of   revenue-generating   advertisement   modules,   and   the   delivery   of   a   seamless   user   experience.    The   Landscape   of   Computerized   Skill   Tests   in   Indian   Recruitment    The   Staff   Selection   Commission   (SSC)   remains   the   largest   orchestrator   of   typing   assessments   through   the   Data   Entry   Speed   Test   (DEST),   which   is   mandatory   for   final   selection   in   numerous   Group   B   and   C   posts. 1   The   evaluation   of   a   candidate’s   performance   in   the   DEST   is   not   based   on   a   raw   word-per-minute   (WPM)   score   but   on   a   sophisticated   calculation   of   errors,   categorized   with   surgical   precision   into   "Full"   and   "Half"   mistakes. 3   Failure   to   meet   the   mandatory   accuracy   threshold   results   in   immediate   disqualification,   regardless   of   the   candidate’s   performance   in   previous   written   tiers. 1   For   the   Combined   Graduate   Level   (CGL)   examination,   the   requirement   typically   centers   on   2000   key   depressions   within   a   15-minute   window,   whereas   the   Combined   Higher   Secondary   Level   (CHSL)   demands   a   higher   throughput   of   35   WPM   in   English   or   30   WPM   in   Hindi. 2    In   parallel,   the   Railway   Recruitment   Board   (RRB)   NTPC   (Non-Technical   Popular   Categories)    typing   tests   introduce   a   distinct   marking   formula   characterized   by   a   heavy   "10-word   penalty"   for   every   error   committed   beyond   a   5%   buffer. 7   This   necessitates   a   training   platform   that   allows   users   to   toggle   between   these   marking   systems,   as   the   strategy   for   passing   an   SSC   exam   (where   accuracy   thresholds   are   broader)   differs   significantly   from   the   RRB   environment,   where   a   single   mistake   can   decimate   a   high-speed   performance   score. 8
